2) Scope

This Policy applies to personal data processed when you access or use the CATE mobile app and related services. It does not apply to third-party websites/services you may access through links or integrations; those are governed by their own policies.

3) Information we collect

3.1 Information you provide

  • Account identifiers: email address and/or phone number; authentication data (e.g., one-time password verification status).
  • Profile data: name or nickname, age/date of birth, gender, dating preferences, photos, bio, career-related details (e.g., role/industry), values/interests, and other info you choose to share.
  • User content: messages, attachments/media, and any other content you submit or transmit through the Service.
  • Support and safety: reports you submit, information you provide to customer support, appeals, and communications.
  • Optional information: any additional profile fields you choose to complete (always optional unless marked required in-app).

3.2 Information we collect automatically

  • Device information: device model, operating system version, app version, language, time zone, network and carrier information, and similar device signals.
  • Identifiers: app instance identifiers; and where permitted, advertising identifiers (e.g., Google Advertising ID) used for ads measurement and (if enabled) personalization.
  • Usage data: feature usage, interactions (e.g., likes, swipes, matches), session timestamps, and settings selections.
  • Log & diagnostics: crash logs, performance metrics, and error reports.
  • Approximate or precise location (if enabled): to support distance-based discovery, safety controls, and fraud prevention signals.

3.3 Information from other sources

  • Other users: information provided in reports, blocks, and communications that reference you.
  • Service providers: security, integrity, ads, and analytics vendors may provide risk signals or aggregated measurement (typically non-identifying or pseudonymous where feasible).
  • App stores: limited subscription/purchase status and transaction metadata (we do not receive full payment card details from app stores).

4) How we use information

We use personal data to operate, secure, and improve CATE, including to:

  • Provide the Service: create and manage accounts, enable profiles, matching, chat, and core features.
  • Personalize discovery: recommend profiles and content based on preferences, compatibility signals, and activity patterns.
  • Messaging delivery: deliver messages, compute unread counts, generate inbox summaries, and send push notifications.
  • Safety & integrity: detect and prevent spam, fraud, impersonation, and abusive behavior; enforce our Terms; respond to reports.
  • Customer support: respond to inquiries, troubleshoot issues, and resolve disputes.
  • Analytics & improvement: measure performance, fix bugs, develop new features, and improve reliability.
  • Advertising & measurement: show ads (including via AdMob), measure ad performance, limit ad frequency, and prevent ad fraud.
  • Legal compliance: comply with lawful requests and obligations; protect rights, safety, and property.

8) Messaging, content processing, and encryption

CATE provides chat and messaging features. CATE does not offer end-to-end encryption for messages. Messages are transmitted from the client and stored on our backend to enable delivery, moderation, safety features, and notifications.

8.1 How messaging works in CATE

  • Storage: messages are stored as documents in a database (e.g., Firestore) associated with user accounts and conversations.
  • Processing: server logic (e.g., Cloud Functions) may process message metadata/content to support features like push notifications, unread counts, and inbox summaries.
  • Mirroring: message events may be mirrored into each user’s inbox summary to enable server-side delivery logic such as unread counters and push notifications.

8.2 Content access

We may access, review, or use automated tools to analyze messages/content in limited circumstances, including: (a) to investigate reports and enforce Terms, (b) to comply with legal obligations, (c) to prevent spam/fraud/abuse, and (d) to ensure technical delivery and feature functionality.

Please avoid sharing sensitive information (e.g., passwords, financial account details) in chat. Other users can copy, screenshot, or save content you share.
